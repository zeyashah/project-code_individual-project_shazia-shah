The International Museum of Tiles has the mission of collecting, preserving, studying and disseminating representative samples of the evolution of Ceramics and Tiles in the world, promoting good practices in Inventorying, Documentation, Research, Classification, Disclosure, Conservation and Restoration of Ceramics. in particular, the Tile. The mission of the MNAz also includes the patrimonial safeguard of the church and other spaces of the former Monastery where the museum is housed.


The IMOT seeks to establish itself as a national and international reference, because of the specificity of its collections and museum spaces, as well as the quality of the knowledge that it is responsible for producing and supporting.


The Center of its activities is Ceramics Coating, which functions as an entity of reference and support for the academic and professional class. The scientific and technological investigations in the areas of the ceramic coating, support the public and private entities which protect heritage buildings built with ceramic tiles, all over the world.


Through its activities, the museum reveals the history of the civilizations in different regions of the world, seeking to draw the attention of society to the need and importance of protecting what is the artistic expression that differentiates culture.
